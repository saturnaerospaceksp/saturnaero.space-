About Rhea & Iapetus

Rhea and Iapetus are built as a practical launcher family, balancing reliable reuse with a mission profile that can stay in service across a wide range of payloads and orbital demands.

The stack is designed to keep operations straightforward while still giving Saturn Aerospace the flexibility to move quickly between missions without rebuilding the vehicle around each launch.

OPERATIONAL ADVANTAGES

Why This Stack Works

  • Recovery and reuse are built into the vehicle plan from the start.
  • Rapid turnaround keeps the launch flow moving without unnecessary downtime.
  • A common stack reduces integration friction across missions.
  • Flexible enough to support both routine and more demanding launch profiles.

THE STACK

The Hotstage

The hotstage delivers a 40% more efficient separation, leading to more payload mass reaching orbit.

It is part of what makes the stack feel composed and repeatable: less drama on the pad, cleaner loads in flight, and a smoother path from launch to payload deployment.

WHY IT MATTERS

Why The Stack Works

  • Cleaner stage connection keeps the architecture tidy through the handoff between stages.
  • 40% more efficient separation improves staging performance and leaves more payload capability for orbit.
  • Flexible across profiles so the vehicle stays useful across a broad range of mission needs and payload types.

THE NEXT STEP

Rhea To The Moon

The Rhea design team are looking at ways to bring Rhea to the moon in 2026. With a fully modified version of the Rhea second stage, we can provide reliable access to the moon with crew and supplies.
